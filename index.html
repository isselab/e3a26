What this workshop is for

A serious conversation beyond agent demos.

The focus is not hype. The focus is what engineering organizations can actually deploy, measure, govern, and trust.

  • Operational adoption Tooling, workflows, rollout strategies, and organizational readiness.
  • Industrial evidence What works in practice, what breaks, and how teams evaluate outcomes.
  • Research with teeth Academic perspectives grounded in delivery, quality, governance, and impact.
Why Now

Software teams are entering the agentic era without a clear playbook.

AI-assisted development is already changing how code is written, reviewed, tested, and maintained. Agentic workflows raise a harder question: how should engineering organizations integrate these systems responsibly into real software delivery?

E3A is designed as a focused European forum for discussing the technical, organizational, and strategic implications of agentic software engineering. The workshop will emphasize lessons that matter for industry adoption: evaluation, reliability, compliance, developer experience, architecture, and the realities of deploying AI-native tooling in production environments.

Industry-first does not mean research-light. It means the conversation starts with real engineering constraints and uses research to sharpen, challenge, and improve the path forward.

The organizers come from software engineering research, but the workshop is intentionally shaped to attract decision-makers, tool builders, and experienced practitioners from industry.

Focus Areas

The agenda centers on production concerns, not surface-level novelty.

Topics will evolve with invited participants, but the current emphasis is on where agentic systems meet software engineering practice at scale.

Delivery

Agentic coding workflows

How autonomous and semi-autonomous agents fit into implementation, code review, refactoring, and test generation.

Assurance

Evaluation and trust

Benchmarks, observability, failure modes, and evidence for when an agentic setup is safe to rely on.

Governance

Control, compliance, accountability

Policies, approvals, traceability, and responsibility boundaries when AI starts acting inside delivery pipelines.

Architecture

Enterprise integration

Connecting agentic systems to repositories, tickets, tests, docs, internal knowledge, and secure development workflows.

People

Developer experience and team design

What changes for engineering teams, platform groups, productivity functions, and technical leadership.

Outcomes

Value beyond headline productivity

Quality, speed, knowledge flow, maintainability, and the metrics that matter once pilots become operating practice.
